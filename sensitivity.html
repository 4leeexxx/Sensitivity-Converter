FPS Mouse Sensitivity Converter — Valorant, CS2, Fortnite & More

Switching games but want to keep the same crosshair feel? Our free sensitivity converter translates your sensitivity across all major FPS titles including Valorant, CS2, Fortnite, Apex Legends, Overwatch 2, Rainbow Six Siege, Warzone, and more — using each game's exact yaw (degrees-per-count) value for pixel-perfect accuracy.

What Is eDPI?

eDPI (effective DPI) = DPI × in-game sensitivity. It is the universal measure of how fast your crosshair moves regardless of game. Most pro players sit between 200–400 eDPI. Knowing your eDPI lets you match your feel perfectly across any title.

What Is cm/360°?

The centimetres you move your mouse to make a full 360° turn in-game. The most platform-agnostic way to compare sensitivities. Tactical FPS pros typically use 30–50 cm/360°.

Frequently Asked Questions

Most pro FPS players use low sensitivity. Valorant pros average 0.2–0.4 at 800 DPI (~200–350 eDPI). CS2 pros typically use 1.0–2.0 at 400 DPI.
Some games add multipliers for ADS, scope, or FOV. This converter matches base hip-fire sensitivity as closely as mathematically possible.
Most players find 20–50 cm/360° comfortable. Tactical shooters favour 30–50 cm/360° for precision. Battle royales trend lower for faster flick shots.
